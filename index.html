1.　Where is Hiroshima and how to access?

Hiroshima prefecture is located at west side of Japan and just halfway between Osaka and Fukuoka.

Historycaly, this place ware so populated and regarded as an ordinance-designated city now.

In this place, there are two world Cultural Heritage and they attract not only domestic visitors but also foreign tourist.

Hiroshima is famous for atomic bomb dorm, right?

Right. Atomic bomb dorm and Itukushima temple are recorded as a World Cultural Heritage.

Needless to say, former one is the building destroyed by Atomic bomb in 1945 and accepted as a World Cultural Heritage in 1996.

Later one is a historical shrine made in 6c and recorded as a World Cultural Heritage in 1996. I'll give more details later.

Both of them are so famous as to be picked up by textbook for Japanese primary school.

4.　Miyazima：Itukushima temple and Misen climbing

Itukushima Shrine is the second World Cultural Heritage in Hiroshima.

One clan "Saeki no Kuramoto"(佐伯鞍職) decided to build this shrine in order to worship

"Ichibintoshima Hime!"(市杵嶋姫命) Which is regarded as the god of water at end of 6c. Around 12c,

famous military commander "Kiyomori Taira" refurbished this shrine and it made Itukushima shrine renowned.

Why was this shrine built in the sea?

Is there specific reason?

Off course! The first reason is that people in this era regarded Miyazima(Island)

as a sacred place, therefore they ware afraid of building Shrine on the surface of the

Island. Secondly, as I mentioned, they believe the existence of the god of the water.

This shrine play a role to connect Human and God of water.

I see. Sound interesting.

Entrance fee is about $3.

The inside of the shrine is spacious, and you can walk around freely.
